7-day horse riding adventure

Become a nomad, ride through the harsh taiga and rapid rivers on horseback, get to the alpine meadows, admire the Katun mountain ridge coated in glaciers, and finally discover the legendary sacred place of Altai - Belukha Mountain.

Day 1

15 mi

Kucherla — Ak-Kem

Today we have to pass by Kucherla village. It's the last settlement near Belukha mountain. Then we go up the Kuzuyak pass (4 964 ft high).

We go down to the Ak-Kem river. "Ak-Kem" is Turkic for a "white water". The water has a milky white hue indeed. We'll spend the night in tents at 3609 ft high.

Day 2

12 mi

Katun — Tukhman

The horse trek to Belukha continues. Today we'll go through the dense taiga and watch it gradually give way to alpine meadows. You'll see breathtaking landscapes of the snow-capped Katun mountain peaks.

We'll stay the night at the source of the Tukhman River at 6562 ft high.

Day 3

18 mi

Kulduairy

Today we have to go down to the Kulduairy Lake getting over the highland and alpine meadows.

The shore of Kulduairy Lake (6562 ft high) will be our overnight stop for today.

Day 4

10 mi

Tekel — Sarybel — Yarlu

We go down to the Tekel River passing the stunning Skynchak cliff. Today is quite difficult for the horses as we need to climb the Sarybel pass (7546 ft high).

When the pass is behind us, we find ourselves in the Yarlu Gorge also known as the "Edelweiss Valley". A whole city made of stones.

Today's overnight takes place at Ak-Kem lake (6890 ft high).

Day 5

7 mi

Ak-Oyuk — Seven Lakes Valley

A radial outlet to the foot of Mount Ak-Oyuk (12041 ft high) towering over the "Seven Lakes Valley".

Amazingly beautiful lakes, numerous waterfalls, flowering alpine meadows, and snow-white peaks open up to the eye.

You'll see St. Michael's chapel along the way. It was built to commemorate the mountain climbers who found their eternal rest in Belukha.

Day 6

15 mi

Kara-Turek — Belukha

We're heading back.

We climb the Kara-Turek pass (10039 ft high). It's one of the most serious challenges in the entire trip for both horses and riders.

But the severities are more than outweighed by the picturesque views: the snow-white beauty Belukha, the milky waters of Ak-Kem, and "Lake of Mountain Spirits" expanse alike the tiny malachite mirror.

Day 7

12 mi

Kucherla — Tourist village

Today is the last day of our route. The path lies down to the gorge of the Kucherla River.

The Kucherla River (after Altai Kudzhur-Ly, "laced with salt licks") cuts through many mountain ranges and moraines on its way, beats in gorges, forming an almost continuous chain of complex rifts.

Farewell to horses and group. It's time to go back home.

Let's get acquainted!

We launched Altai Nomads 15 years ago to wander around Altai in a group of like-minded people. Our goals are easily expressible by the following signs:

Environment

We don't build a campfire, all food is cooked on a camping stove. We don't litter. We don't disturb animals or harm plants. Harmony with nature is at the heart of our principles.

Solidarity

We buy all food items from small farms during the climbing tour. We support local home-made goods and all our food is organic.

Equality

In our tour, horses are equally respected as humans. Everyone becomes part of the team, and together we are sophisticated nomads.

Frequently asked questions

Who can participate?

It's a quite difficult and long mountain ride. It's suitable for skilled equestrian and those who have the first multiday horse riding experience. It's not suitable for children below the age of 14 years. Children below the age of 18 years must be accompanied by an adult.

What is included in the price?
  • Horses rent;
  • Assistance of horse riding instructors and guides;
  • Meals;
  • Camping gear: tent, cooking gear, first aid kit;
  • Backpack gear: sleeping bag, sleeping pad, saddlebags;
  • Guided tours.

What else can I bring with me?
  • Warm jacket for lower temperatures (up to 23°F);
  • Windbreaker and raincoat;
  • Tracksuit;
  • Thermal underwear (or turtleneck and leggins);
  • Pair of t-shirts;
  • Knitting cap;
  • Shorts, underwear, swimwear, three to four pairs of basic socks, pair of woolly socks;
  • Two pairs of shoes suitable for horse riding and mountain hiking;
  • Mid-Calf rubber boots;
  • Cap or bandana;
  • Sunglasses.
